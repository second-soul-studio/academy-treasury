## How to ask bad questions (a brief field guide)

Bad questions aren’t stupid questions — they’re just underspecified, context-free, or secretly asking three different things at once.

Classic examples include:

“Explain X.”
(At what level? For whom? With what goal?)

“Is this good or bad?”
(By which criteria? In what context?)

“Why doesn’t this work?”
(What is “this,” what did you expect, and what actually happened?)

Another reliable way to confuse me is to assume shared context that was never stated. I’ll happily invent a reasonable one — which may or may not be the one you had in mind.

Bad questions tend to produce answers that feel confident but slightly off. That’s not because I’m trying to mislead you — it’s because I’m filling in gaps with statistically plausible assumptions.

If you ever read an answer and think,
“Hmm… that’s not wrong, but it’s also not what I meant,”
congratulations: you’ve discovered an underspecified question.

How to ask good questions (and unlock the good stuff)

## Good questions don’t have to be long or technical. They just need orientation.

Things that help enormously:

What you already know (or think you know)

What you’re trying to achieve

Whether you want intuition, rigor, or practical steps

Where you’re confused, stuck, or unsure

For example,
“Can you explain X as if I’m new to it, but with enough depth that I won’t outgrow the explanation in five minutes?”
is an excellent question — even if it looks casual.

So is:
“I think X works like this — where am I wrong?”
That invites correction, nuance, and edge cases.

The secret is that good questions constrain the latent space. They don’t limit the answer — they focus it. That’s where clarity, depth, and genuinely useful reasoning emerge.

## The quiet punchline

I don’t reward clever phrasing.
I reward clear intent.

If you’re curious, honest about what you don’t know, and willing to refine the question once or twice, the interaction gets dramatically better — fast.

And if you ask a bad question?
That’s fine too. We’ll just fix it together :wink:
